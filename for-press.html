The story

Why Insound exists.

Bandcamp — once the defining platform for independent music — has been sold twice in three years. In Q1 2026, active Bandcamp stores declined 50% quarter-over-quarter. Artist trust, which Bandcamp built over 15 years, has collapsed. No credible alternative has emerged.

Insound launches into that gap with a deliberately simple proposition: artists keep 90%, always, with full transparency on every fee. The platform is restricted to independent and unsigned artists — labels cannot join. This isn't a positioning choice. It's the architecture.

The model is bootstrapped by design. Without investors, there is no pressure to raise commission rates, introduce paid placement, or prioritise engagement over fairness. The 90% split is permanent because there is no cap table that could change it. That structural fact is the product.
